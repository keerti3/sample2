Eels

Eels are elongated, snake-like fish that inhabit both freshwater and marine environments, often hiding in crevices and ambushing prey with their sharp teeth.

Interesting Facts

  • Electric eels are actually knifefish, and are not in the same order as true eels. They can generate an electric current to stun or kill prey
  • Moray eels are predators that hunt at night, hiding in rocks during the day. They have poor vision, so they use their sense of smell to find prey.
