Flounder

Flounder are flatfish that live on the ocean floor, camouflaging themselves by blending into their sandy or muddy surroundings to ambush prey.

Interesting Facts

  • Flounders are born with eyes on both sides of their heads, but as they mature, one eye gradually migrates to the other side, giving them a unique asymmetrical appearance that helps them lie flat on the ocean floor.
  • Flounders can change their skin color and pattern to blend in with their surroundings, making them nearly invisible to predators and prey on the ocean floor.
  • Flounders are bottom-dwelling fish that spend most of their time lying on their side on the seabed, waiting to ambush prey like small fish and crustaceans.

Importance

Flounder play an important role in marine ecosystems as both predators of smaller marine life and prey for larger species, helping to maintain the balance of the food chain in their ocean habitats.
