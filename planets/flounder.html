The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It provides light, heat, and energy essential for life on Earth. It is a massive ball of hot, glowing gases, primarily hydrogen and helium.

Interesting Facts

  • The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old.
  • It is mostly composed of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%).
  • The Sun's diameter is about 109 times that of Earth.

Importance

The Sun is the primary source of energy in the solar system. It plays a crucial role in sustaining life on Earth and influencing the climate of all the planets.
