REEF EXPLORER 9

Reef Explorer 9 is a two-stage deep-sea exploration vessel designed for the reliable and safe transportation of marine scientists, coral reef monitoring equipment, and research payloads to the worldâ€™s most remote coral reefs.

CORAL TRANSPORT MODULE

The Coral Transport Module is designed to carry sensitive marine life, coral samples, and monitoring tools in its pressurized chamber. The lower, unpressurized section allows for the safe transport of larger oceanographic instruments and secondary research payloads. In the future, this module will accommodate human divers for deep-sea coral reef research. .

REEF SHIELD

The Reef Shield ensures safe passage of equipment to underwater destinations like coral reefs, deep-sea ridges, and marine research zones. Its composite structure protects sensitive equipment during descent to extreme depths.

FIRST STAGE

The first stage of the Reef Explorer 9 features nine hydro-powered engines designed to propel the vessel safely through ocean currents. The hull is made from corrosion-resistant alloys, containing compressed air and liquid oxygen to power the vessel's exploration systems.

PAYLOAD

Reef Explorer 9 can transport a variety of payloads, including coral reef research drones, underwater habitats, and sample collection modules, either inside the Coral Transport Module or attached externally in the unpressurized section.

SECOND STAGE: DEEP SEA NAVIGATOR

The second stage, powered by a single high-efficiency propulsion system, guides the Reef Explorer 9 to its targeted depth. Redundant systems ensure maximum reliability for precision underwater navigation and research operations.

NINE PROPULSION ENGINES

With its nine first-stage engines clustered together, the Reef Explorer 9 can sustain up to two propulsion system shutdowns during a mission and still safely complete its research journey. This key reliability feature makes it one of the most trusted vessels for coral reef exploration..
